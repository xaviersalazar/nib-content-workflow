# Dunkleosteus

Part of [Hall of Vertebrate Origins](https://www.amnh.org/exhibitions/permanent/vertebrate-origins).

![Suspended from the ceiling in the Museum’s Hall of Vertebrate Origins, a model of a Dunkleosteus terrelli. Its open jaw shows its razor-sharp edges.](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

_Dunkleosteus_ _terrelli_ boasted a 20-foot-long body covered with bony plates of armor. Fossil records indicate that this huge fish, one of the first large jawed vertebrates in the ocean, was an aggressive predator.

The razor-sharp edges of bones in this animal's jaws served as cutters. As they rubbed against each other, the opposing jaw blades acted like self-sharpening shears. These bones continued to grow as they were worn down by use.
