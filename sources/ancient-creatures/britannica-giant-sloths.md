**Megatherium**, largest of the ground sloths, an extinct group of mammals belonging to a group containing sloths, anteaters, glyptodonts, and armadillos that underwent a highly successful evolutionary radiation in [South America](https://www.britannica.com/place/South-America) in the [Cenozoic Era](https://www.britannica.com/science/Cenozoic-Era) (beginning 65.5 million years ago). The size of these animals approximated that of a modern [elephant](https://www.britannica.com/animal/elephant-mammal), and they were equipped with large claws and teeth; the latter were [confined](https://www.britannica.com/dictionary/confined) to the sides of the [jaw](https://www.britannica.com/science/jaw), because the [animal](https://www.britannica.com/animal/animal) fed largely on the leaves of trees and bushes. Ground sloths appeared briefly in [North America](https://www.britannica.com/place/North-America) during the [Pleistocene Epoch](https://www.britannica.com/science/Pleistocene-Epoch) (2.6 million to 11,700 years ago), when a land connection was established between the American continents.
