**aptronym**, a name that fits some aspect of a character, as in Mr. Talkative and Mr. Worldly Wiseman in [John Bunyan](https://www.britannica.com/biography/John-Bunyan)’s _The Pilgrim’s Progress_ or [Mrs. Malaprop](https://www.britannica.com/topic/malapropism) in [Richard Brinsley Sheridan](https://www.britannica.com/biography/Richard-Brinsley-Sheridan)’s play _The Rivals._ The term _aptronym_ was allegedly coined by the American newspaper columnist [Franklin P. Adams](https://www.britannica.com/biography/Franklin-Pierce-Adams), by an anagrammatic reordering of the first letters of _patronym_ (to suggest _apt_) to [denote](https://www.britannica.com/dictionary/denote) surnames that suit the occupation of the name’s bearer (such as Baker for a baker). Both _aptronym_ and the synonymous _euonym_ are rarely encountered.
