# Asteroids

![Closeup view of a rocky, diamond-shaped asteroid in space](https://science.nasa.gov/wp-content/uploads/2018/07/bennu-banner-1920x640-1.jpg?w=1920)

## Introduction

Asteroids, sometimes called minor planets, are rocky, airless remnants left over from the early formation of our solar system about 4.6 billion years ago.

Most asteroids can be found orbiting the Sun between Mars and Jupiter within the main asteroid belt. Asteroids range in size from [Vesta](https://science.nasa.gov/solar-system/asteroids/4-vesta/) – the largest at about 329 miles (530 kilometers) in diameter – to bodies that are less than 33 feet (10 meters) across. The total mass of all the asteroids combined is less than that of Earth's Moon.

## Planetary Defense Coordination Office (PDCO)

The PDCO manages NASA's mission to find, track, and better understand asteroids and comets that could pose an impact hazard to Earth.

![Illustration of an asteroid approaching Earth](https://science.nasa.gov/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/cneos-banner.png?w=150&h=150&crop=1)

## Center for Near Earth Object Studies (CNEOS)

CNEOS computes high-precision orbits for Near-Earth Objects (NEOs) in support of NASA’s Planetary Defense Coordination Office.
