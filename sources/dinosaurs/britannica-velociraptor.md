### How large was a _Velociraptor_ compared to a human being?

_Velociraptor_ was somewhat smaller and somewhat lighter than modern adult [human beings](https://www.britannica.com/topic/human-being). The largest members of the genus grew up to length of 1.8 metres (6 feet), which is comparable to the height of a an adult male human, but they probably weighed no more than 45 kg (100 pounds). In contrast, the average weight of an adult human being is about 62 kg (about 137 pounds).

### Why did _Velociraptor_ go extinct?

No one knows for sure why _Velociraptor_ went extinct, but [fossil](https://www.britannica.com/science/fossil) evidence suggests that it died out about 70 million years ago near the end of the [Cretaceous Period](https://www.britannica.com/science/Cretaceous-Period). The timing of their [extinction](https://www.britannica.com/science/extinction-biology) came shortly before the onset of the [K–T extinction](https://www.britannica.com/science/K-T-extinction), the global [mass extinction event](https://www.britannica.com/science/mass-extinction-event) that wiped out the [dinosaurs](https://www.britannica.com/animal/dinosaur) and many other forms of [life](https://www.britannica.com/science/life).

### What animal is a descendant of a Velociraptor?

Modern [birds](https://www.britannica.com/animal/bird-animal) are thought to be closely related to [dromaeosaurs](https://www.britannica.com/animal/dromaeosaur), which is the name of the family in which _Velociraptor_ is classified, but, so far, there is no direct link between _Velociraptor_ and modern birds.

**Velociraptor**, (genus _Velociraptor_), sickle-clawed [dinosaur](https://www.britannica.com/animal/dinosaur) that [flourished](https://www.britannica.com/dictionary/flourished) in central and eastern Asia during the Late [Cretaceous Period](https://www.britannica.com/science/Cretaceous-Period) (99 million to 65 million years ago). It is closely related to the North American _Deinonychus_ of the Early Cretaceous in that both [reptiles](https://www.britannica.com/animal/reptile) were [dromaeosaurs](https://www.britannica.com/animal/dromaeosaur). Both possessed an unusually large [claw](https://www.britannica.com/science/claw) on each foot, as well as ossified [tendon](https://www.britannica.com/science/tendon) reinforcements in the tail that enabled them to maintain balance while striking and slashing at [prey](https://www.britannica.com/science/predation) with one foot upraised. _Velociraptor_ was smaller than _Deinonychus_, reaching a length of only 1.8 metres (6 feet) and perhaps weighing no more than 45 kg (100 pounds). _Velociraptor_ appears to have been a swift, agile predator of small herbivores.
